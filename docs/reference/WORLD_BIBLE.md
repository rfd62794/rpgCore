# rpgCore World Bible
**Designer Agent Lore Dump - Organized Reference v1.0**

---

## 📖 Core Premise

The player is an astronaut who crash lands in a garden. The ship is damaged. The world is unfamiliar. The goal, initially, is to repair the ship and leave.

**The game is realizing you don't want to.**

The garden is the last neutral ground in a fractured world. It is not neutral by politics — it is neutral by nature. It is the last living remnant of the Void, the original unified culture from which all others fractured. The astronaut crashed into the most sacred convergence point in the world without knowing it.

Everyone has an opinion about the astronaut before they understand why.

## 🌍 Hexagon World Structure

```
        Crystal
       /        \
  Tundra          Ember
    |    [GARDEN]    |
  Marsh           Gale
       \        /
        Tide
```

### Opposing Dualities (across the hexagon)
- **Ember ↔ Marsh** — Fire and Water. Aggression versus endurance. The most classical tension.
- **Gale ↔ Tundra** — Wind and Ice. Instinct versus calculation. Motion versus stillness.
- **Crystal ↔ Tide** — Earth and Lightning. Structure versus flow. Isolation versus connection.

### Active Triangle
**Ember, Gale, Marsh** — Fire, Wind, Water. The flowing aggressive elements. They interact through conflict and motion.

### Patient Triangle
**Crystal, Tundra, Tide** — Earth, Ice, Lightning. The structural elements. They interact through trade and endurance.

## 🏛️ The Six Cultures

### Ember — Fire 🔥
- **Stat Affinity**: Strength, Attack
- **Temperament**: Aggressive, impulsive, dominance-seeking
- **Resources**: Scrap (forge and industry)
- **Role in Conflict**: Primary aggressor and accuser. Loudest voice, most militant. Using the fracture crisis to consolidate power. May genuinely believe their own narrative or be cynically exploiting it.
- **Culture Hub**: Forge cities, industrial heat, weapon culture

### Gale — Wind 🌪️
- **Stat Affinity**: Dexterity, Speed
- **Temperament**: Curious, restless, risk-taking
- **Resources**: Light materials, information
- **Role in Conflict**: Fast and connected. Natural information brokers. Allegiances shift with opportunity.
- **Culture Hub**: Elevated settlements, open sky, messenger culture

### Marsh — Water 💧
- **Stat Affinity**: Constitution, Endurance
- **Temperament**: Patient, stubborn, resource-hoarding
- **Resources**: Food (biological abundance)
- **Role in Conflict**: Patient and observant. Probably knows more than they say. Neither aligned nor opposed — watching.
- **Culture Hub**: Wetland settlements, biological richness, healer culture

### Crystal — Earth 💎
- **Stat Affinity**: Defense, Wisdom
- **Temperament**: Cautious, loyal, defensive
- **Resources**: Gems, rare components
- **Role in Conflict**: Natural alliance with Tundra. Wants stability restored. Suspicious of Ember's ambition. Cautious about the astronaut but potentially an early diplomatic win.
- **Culture Hub**: Cavern cities, mineral wealth, scholar culture

### Tundra — Ice ❄️
- **Stat Affinity**: Intelligence, Perception
- **Temperament**: Independent, calculating, emotionally distant
- **Resources**: Preserved materials, rare knowledge
- **Role in Conflict**: Allied with Crystal. Self-sufficient and hard to pressure. Slow to act but decisive when committed.
- **Culture Hub**: Isolated fortresses, preservation culture, ancient knowledge

### Tide — Lightning ⚡
- **Stat Affinity**: Charisma, Adaptability
- **Temperament**: Sociable, adaptable, opportunistic
- **Resources**: Gold (trade and connection)
- **Role in Conflict**: Wildcard. Traders deal with everyone, which means leverage over everyone and allegiance to none. The natural diplomatic hub.
- **Culture Hub**: Tundra trading ports, merchant culture, connector of worlds

## 🌑 The Void — The Seventh

Void is not a sixth or seventh culture in the same sense as the others.

Void is what existed before the schism. The original unified culture from which all six fractured. Black does not mean absence — it means all colors unified. A prism splits white light into spectrum. The six cultures are the spectrum. Void is the light before it was divided.

The other cultures fear Void not because it is evil but because it reminds them of what they lost and what they did. Guilt externalized as prejudice. Ember is loudest about it because Ember is furthest from the original nature.

**Void slimes are not recruited from a culture hub. They are bred across generations. The garden is the only place with the conditions to produce them intentionally.**

A Void slime appearing is a world event. Culture hubs send emissaries when one is reported.

## 🗺️ The Six Intersection Zones

Between neighboring cultures on the hexagon, wilderness zones form at the intersection of two elemental natures. These are not settled — they are the true wilderness of the world.

| Zone | Cultures | Elements | Character |
|------|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Magma** | Ember + Crystal | Fire + Earth | Industrial, creative, most contested. Best weapons come from here. |
| **Firestorm** | Ember + Gale | Fire + Wind | Devastating, explosive, unstable. High risk dispatch territory. |
| **Squall** | Gale + Tide | Wind + Lightning | Fast, unpredictable. Information and contraband move through here. |
| **Storm** | Tide + Marsh | Lightning + Water | Volatile but fertile. Exotic organic resources. Dangerous to traverse. |
| **Bog** | Marsh + Tundra | Water + Ice | Dark, slow, ancient. Secrets and ruins. Largely unexplored. |
| **Frost** | Tundra + Crystal | Ice + Earth | Cold and hard. Inhospitable. Most ancient ruins live here. |

Void slimes are most commonly encountered in these intersection zones — where the elemental balance is most stressed, the original unified nature bleeds through.

## 🌿 The Garden

The Garden is home base. The player's anchor. The only safe place in an unfamiliar world.

It is not neutral by politics. It is neutral by nature — the last living convergence point of all six elements. The Garden does not belong to any culture because it belongs to all of them equally, and to Void most of all.

**What the Garden is:**
- Where slimes are born, raised, trained, and age
- Where the player lives and recovers between expeditions
- Where the burnt ship sits as a constant reminder of the original purpose
- Where relationships form through the conversation system
- The emotional center of everything

**What the Garden is not:**
- A menu screen
- A safe room between action sequences  
- Capturable by any culture (this is an uneasy agreement all six cultures honor)

The Garden expands over time. New rooms unlock. The nursery becomes a clinic, a training hall, a vault, a war room, a marketplace. But it always feels like home.

## 📖 Narrative Entry

The player wakes disoriented in the Garden. Something is already there. It becomes the player's first anchor to this world — first source of information, first relationship, first understanding that leaving may be more complicated than finding replacement parts.

The ship repair arc provides initial purpose and structure. Finding or building replacement parts requires scrap, knowledge, and access to cultures that may not be welcoming. The ship is always visible in the garden. The player can always see what they came from.

The game is realizing the garden matters more than the ship.

## 💰 Resources

Three essential resources flow through the world:

| Resource | Source | Purpose |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Gold** | Tide culture, trade routes | Liquid economy. Hiring, buying equipment, trading between cultures. |
| **Scrap** | Ember culture, wilderness salvage | Material economy. Building, repairing, crafting. Ship repair requires scrap. |
| **Food** | Marsh culture, foraging zones | Living world economy. Feeding the roster, sustaining territory, fueling expeditions. |

Culture-specific rare resources exist beyond these three and become available through diplomatic standing with each hub.

## 🎭 Tone

The world is in conflict but the game is not about war. It is about a stranger becoming part of something they didn't intend to join. The slimes are the player's hands in the world. The astronaut is the player's heart.

The tone drifts constantly — that is the design. You came to leave. You stayed to understand. You're still here because something grew.

---

## 🔄 World State Dynamics

### The Fracture Event
The world is in turmoil. The cultures have always vied for dominance, but a fracture event — occurring at or near the time of the crash — has tipped a cold war into open conflict. The astronaut did not cause the turmoil. But they may have accelerated it. The distinction matters and the player may never fully know which is true.

Ember uses the ambiguity as accusation. They point at the astronaut, point at the rift, and the narrative writes itself.

### Cultural Memory
Factions remember what you did. Choices made while surviving have consequences you did not anticipate. Alignment in Act 1 determines who trusts you in Act 3.

### The Economy
Not your clan. The existing civilization. Clans have territory, resources, relationships, and memory. Your garden sits in contested territory. The crash was not random. Something brought you here. The clans know what it was. You do not. Yet.
